Playing Brighter Worlds

Oh no! You've been asked to play some TTRPG system that isn't D&D and you aren't familiar with it! What is Brighter Worlds?!

Worry not! No preparation is technically necessary as the first part of the session I will help everyone create or generate characters, as well as explain the basic rules. If you have played another TTRPG system before, you shouldn’t have too much trouble picking up Brighter Worlds.

Brighter Worlds is a lightweight, exploration-focused RPG that strips things down to the essentials: no classes, levels, or hit points — just three core stats (Strength, Dexterity, Willpower), simple d4–d12 rolls, and fast, consequence-driven decisions. Combat is quick and deadly (most enemies go down in a hit or two), so creative problem-solving and clever roleplay are key. Magic is flexible and often risky, but anyone can try it if they’ve found the right scroll. Character options come from modular “Callings” that give you unique powers without needing to learn the whole system. It’s great for story-first play, fast sessions, and players who enjoy discovery, interaction, and improvising their way through a whimsical but dangerous world.

If, in the interest of time, you do want to learn more ahead of time, you can read the full Brighter Worlds rules online.

If you want to create a character, you can use Brighter Worlds Online, which is a tool I’ve created for managing Brighter Worlds characters. There are a couple of videos explaining how to create a character and how to use the character sheet in Brighter Worlds Online.
